SCHEME NAME
Rythu Bima — Farmers Group Life Insurance Scheme
Also called: Rythu Bheema, Farmer Group Life Insurance Scheme

TYPE
State Government — Telangana
Implemented through Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC)

WHAT IT IS
A free life insurance scheme for farmers. If a registered farmer dies for any reason — natural death, accident, or illness — their family receives Rs.5 lakh within 10 days. The entire insurance premium is paid by the Telangana government. The farmer pays nothing.

BENEFIT AMOUNT
- Rs.5,00,000 (five lakh rupees) paid to the nominee on the death of the farmer
- Covers death due to any reason — natural, accidental, illness
- Amount transferred directly to nominee's bank account via RTGS within 10 days
- Premium paid entirely by Telangana government — zero cost to farmer

WHO IS ELIGIBLE
- Farmers who are permanent residents of Telangana
- Age between 18 and 59 years
- Must be a Pattadar Passbook holder (land registered in farmer's name in Dharani database)
- Only one policy per farmer

WHO IS NOT ELIGIBLE
- Farmers aged 60 years and above
- Tenant farmers (land not in their own name)
- Farmers not registered in Dharani / Bhubharati land records

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR ENROLMENT
- Pattadar Passbook
- Aadhaar Card
- Residence proof
- Nominee's Aadhaar Card
- Nominee's bank account details
- Mobile number

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR CLAIM (after farmer's death)
- Death Certificate of the farmer
- Aadhaar Card of deceased farmer
- Aadhaar Card of nominee
- Pattadar Passbook copy
- Nominee's bank account passbook copy
- Any additional documents requested by LIC

HOW TO ENROL
Step 1 — Contact your local Agriculture Extension Officer (AEO) — you do NOT need to visit any office yourself
Step 2 — AEO collects your personal details, land details, and nominee details
Step 3 — AEO enters your data into the Rythu Bima online portal
Step 4 — Mandal Agriculture Officer verifies and authenticates the data
Step 5 — LIC generates a unique LIC ID for the farmer
Step 6 — Farmer receives an SMS on registered mobile with LIC ID
Step 7 — LIC issues a Certificate of Insurance (Annexure II) in the farmer's name

HOW TO CLAIM (after farmer's death)
Step 1 — Nominee informs the District Nodal Officer through the Rythu Bima portal — no office visit needed
Step 2 — Nominee uploads: death certificate, ID proofs, and bank details on the portal
Step 3 — AEO and Mandal Agriculture Officer verify the claim
Step 4 — Verified claim forwarded to LIC
Step 5 — LIC transfers Rs.5 lakh to nominee's bank account within 10 days
IMPORTANT — Claim must be made within 6 months of the farmer's death
