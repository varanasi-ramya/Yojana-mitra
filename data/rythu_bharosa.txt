SCHEME NAME
Rythu Bharosa (formerly known as Rythu Bandhu)
Also called: Agriculture Investment Support Scheme, FISS (Farmer Investment Support Scheme)
Renamed from Rythu Bandhu to Rythu Bharosa on 26 January 2025 by CM A. Revanth Reddy

TYPE
State Government — Telangana Agriculture and Farmer Welfare Department

WHAT IT IS
A direct financial support scheme that gives money to farmers every crop season to buy seeds, fertilizers, pesticides and other inputs — so they do not need to take loans. Money is paid directly to the farmer's bank account before each season.

BENEFIT AMOUNT
- Rs.12,000 per acre per year (revised amount as of 2025)
- Paid in two equal instalments of Rs.6,000 each
- First instalment: before Kharif season (June)
- Second instalment: before Rabi season (December)
- No upper cap on number of acres
- Payment mode: Direct Bank Transfer (DBT) to farmer's registered bank account

WHO IS ELIGIBLE
- Farmers who are permanent residents of Telangana
- Farmers who own agricultural land registered in their name in Bhubharati / Dharani land records portal
- Small and marginal farmers
- Large landholders (no acreage cap)
- Farmers with Forest Rights Record (ROFR) documents
- Land must be cultivable agricultural land that is actively farmed

WHO IS NOT ELIGIBLE
- Tenant farmers (land not in their name) — they are covered under Indiramma Atmiya Bharosa scheme instead
- Farmers whose land is non-cultivable (mining areas, industrial zones, real estate land)
- Income tax payers
- Government employees
- Farmers residing outside Telangana

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
- Aadhaar Card (mandatory)
- Pattadar Passbook / Bhubharati land ownership document
- Bank account passbook (account must be in farmer's own name)
- Residence proof
- Ration card
- Mobile number linked to Aadhaar

HOW TO APPLY
Offline:
Step 1 — Visit your nearest Gram Panchayat / Gram Sabha office OR a Praja Palana Centre / Common Service Centre (CSC)
Step 2 — Collect the Rythu Bharosa application form (free of cost)
Step 3 — Fill the form with your land details, Aadhaar number, and bank account details
Step 4 — Attach required documents
Step 5 — Submit at the Gram Panchayat or CSC
Step 6 — Payment is made via DBT directly to your bank account before each season

Online:
Visit rythubharosa.telangana.gov.in
Go to Farmer's Corner
Select Rythu Bharosa application
Enter Aadhaar, land details, and upload documents

HOW TO CHECK PAYMENT STATUS
Visit rythubharosa.telangana.gov.in — Check Amount — enter Aadhaar number

IF PAYMENT NOT RECEIVED
- Contact your local Mandal Revenue Officer (MRO) or Agriculture Extension Officer (AEO)
- Verify your land is registered in Bhubharati portal
- Ensure your bank account is Aadhaar-linked
