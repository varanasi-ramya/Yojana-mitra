SCHEME NAME
Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
Also called: KCC, Kisan Card, Farmer Credit Card

TYPE
Central Government scheme
Implemented by all banks — nationalised banks, cooperative banks, and Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)

WHAT IT IS
A short-term revolving credit card given to farmers to buy seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, and other inputs without depending on moneylenders. The interest rate is very low — only 4% per year with government subsidy. After harvest, repay the amount. Before next season, borrow again. Works like a revolving credit limit.

BENEFIT / CREDIT LIMIT
- Credit limit based on land holding and crop — typically Rs.50,000 to Rs.3,00,000
- Interest rate: 4% per year for loans up to Rs.3 lakh (with 2% interest subvention from Central Government plus 3% prompt repayment incentive)
- Flexible withdrawal — borrow as much as needed up to the limit, repay after harvest
- Covers: seed purchase, fertilizer, pesticide, post-harvest expenses, farm maintenance
- Also covers allied activities: animal husbandry, fisheries, dairy
- Personal accident insurance of Rs.50,000 (death or permanent disability) included free with KCC

WHO IS ELIGIBLE
- All farmers — individual or joint borrowers
- Tenant farmers, oral lessees, and sharecroppers
- Self Help Groups (SHGs) of farmers
- Fishermen — for purchase of boats, nets, and fishing inputs
- Animal husbandry and dairy farmers

WHO IS NOT ELIGIBLE
- Non-farmers and urban residents
- Farmers with existing loan default on agriculture accounts (case by case at bank's discretion)

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
- Aadhaar Card
- PAN Card
- Pattadar Passbook / land ownership documents
- 2 passport-size photographs
- Bank account details
- Address proof
- For tenant farmers: tenancy or lease agreement

HOW TO APPLY
Step 1 — Visit your nearest bank branch — any nationalised bank (SBI, Union Bank, Andhra Bank etc.), cooperative bank, or Regional Rural Bank
Step 2 — Ask for KCC application form — it is completely free
Step 3 — Fill in your land details, crop details, and estimated seasonal farming expenses
Step 4 — Submit form with all required documents
Step 5 — Bank verifies your land records
Step 6 — Credit limit is sanctioned and KCC card is issued within 14 days
Step 7 — Use the card or passbook to withdraw money as needed during the crop season
Step 8 — After harvest, repay the amount to keep the interest rate low

Online option:
Visit pmkisan.gov.in — KCC Saturation Drive — apply online
Or apply through your bank's own website or mobile app

IMPORTANT TIPS
- Always repay on time — prompt repayment earns a 3% additional subvention, making the effective interest rate just 4% per year
- Without prompt repayment, the actual interest rate is 7% per year
- KCC is renewed every year — visit the bank branch before each crop season to renew
- If land is jointly owned, all co-owners must sign the application
- You can use KCC for allied activities (poultry, dairy, fishery) in addition to crop farming
