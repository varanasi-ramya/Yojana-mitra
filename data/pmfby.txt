SCHEME NAME
PMFBY — Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana
Also called: PM Crop Insurance, Fasal Bima Yojana, Prime Minister Crop Insurance Scheme

TYPE
Central Government — Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare
Implemented through empanelled insurance companies in each state

WHAT IT IS
A crop insurance scheme that compensates farmers when crops fail due to natural calamities like floods, drought, hailstorm, cyclone, pest attacks, or disease. Farmers pay a very small premium — the government pays the remaining 80 to 95 percent of the total premium.

BENEFIT / COVERAGE
- Full compensation for crop loss due to: drought, flood, cyclone, hailstorm, landslide, natural fire, lightning, pest and disease outbreak
- Post-harvest losses covered for 14 days after harvest
- Localised calamities like hailstorm and landslide affecting specific farms are covered
- Sum insured is based on crop type and area — set by state government each season
- Compensation paid directly to farmer's bank account via DBT

PREMIUM RATES (farmer's share only)
- Kharif crops (rice, maize, cotton, soyabean): 2% of sum insured
- Rabi crops (wheat, mustard, gram): 1.5% of sum insured
- Horticulture and commercial crops: 5% of sum insured
- Government pays remaining premium — can be 80 to 95% of total premium

WHO IS ELIGIBLE
- All farmers — both loanee and non-loanee farmers
- Farmers growing notified crops in notified areas
- Tenant and sharecropper farmers are also eligible
- Loanee farmers (those with crop loans from banks) are automatically enrolled unless they opt out

WHO IS NOT ELIGIBLE
- Farmers growing non-notified crops in that season and district
- Farmers who miss the application deadline for that season

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
- Aadhaar Card
- Land ownership documents / Pattadar Passbook
- Bank account details / passbook
- Sowing certificate from Village Revenue Officer or Agriculture Extension Officer (AEO)
- For tenant farmers: tenancy or sharecropping agreement

HOW TO APPLY

For loanee farmers (those with crop loans from a bank):
Step 1 — You are automatically enrolled when you take a crop loan from a bank
Step 2 — Premium is deducted from your loan amount by the bank
Step 3 — Check with your bank to confirm enrolment and get your policy number
Step 4 — You can opt out if you do not want insurance — inform bank before deadline

For non-loanee farmers:
Step 1 — Visit your nearest bank branch, CSC, or insurance company agent before the season deadline
Step 2 — Fill the PMFBY proposal form with your crop and land details
Step 3 — Pay your share of premium (2% for Kharif, 1.5% for Rabi)
Step 4 — Receive a policy number — save it carefully

Online:
Visit pmfby.gov.in — Farmer Corner — Apply for Crop Insurance

IMPORTANT DEADLINE
Must apply before the cutoff date for each season — usually 2 to 3 weeks before sowing begins
Missing the deadline means you cannot be insured for that season

HOW TO CLAIM AFTER CROP LOSS
Step 1 — Report within 72 hours of crop damage
Step 2 — Call PMFBY helpline 1800-180-1551 OR inform your AEO or local agriculture office
Step 3 — Upload photos of crop damage on the Crop Insurance app if possible
Step 4 — Government officials conduct Crop Cutting Experiments (CCE) to assess loss
Step 5 — Compensation is calculated based on the assessment
Step 6 — Money transferred to your bank account via DBT

For mid-season losses due to flood or drought:
Report immediately to AEO or insurance company — do not wait for harvest
